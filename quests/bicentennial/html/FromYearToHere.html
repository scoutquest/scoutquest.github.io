What word connects all of Tennessee?

VOLUNTEER

The Volunteer State.
Tennessee earned its nickname during the War of 1812, when an overwhelming number of Tennesseans volunteered to join the fight—so many, in fact, that the name stuck. That spirit of service didn’t stop there. From the Mexican-American War to moments of disaster relief and civic duty, Tennesseans have consistently stepped forward when called. “Volunteer” isn’t just a name—it’s a legacy of commitment, pride, and readiness to serve that continues to define the state today.
